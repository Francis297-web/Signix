About Signix

Signix is a deaf-first learning platform built to help anyone achieve fluency in Kenyan Sign Language.

We believe KSL should be accessible to everyone — deaf learners, parents of deaf children, teachers, interpreters, and allies. Like w3schools gives structure for code, and Khan Academy gives progression for math, Signix gives both for KSL.

Our Mission

Break down barriers to KSL fluency. No audio required. No assumptions. Just clear video cards, organized categories, and a revision system that respects how deaf people actually learn.

👋

Deaf-First Design

Every feature is built visual-first. Large buttons, high contrast, no audio dependency. KSL videos with text gloss in English + Kiswahili.

📚

Structured Learning

Categories from Greetings to Workplace KSL. Follow the Fluency Path from Beginner to Advanced, or jump to what you need.

🔄

Smart Revision

Spaced repetition brings back signs you’re about to forget. Add any card to your Revision Deck and master it over time.

Meet the Founder

PW

Patience Wambui

Founder & Lead Advocate, Signix

Patience started Signix after seeing how hard it was for deaf students and their families to find structured, accessible KSL resources online. Her vision: a platform where KSL is taught with the same clarity and rigor as any other language — but designed by and for the deaf community.

How Signix Works

1. Learn by Category

Pick a topic like Family or Numbers. Each video card shows a native signer demonstrating the sign, plus English/Kiswahili gloss. Hover to preview, click to practice.

2. Build Your Revision Deck

Struggling with a sign? Click “Add to Revision”. Signix will remind you to review it later using spaced repetition — the same method used for medical school and language learning.

3. Follow the Fluency Path

Complete Beginner categories to unlock Intermediate. Master Intermediate to reach Advanced. Track your progress and celebrate milestones.

Community & Accuracy

KSL has regional dialects and evolving usage. Signix partners with native KSL signers to review all content. If you’re a fluent signer, you’ll soon be able to submit variant signs for moderation through our community portal.
